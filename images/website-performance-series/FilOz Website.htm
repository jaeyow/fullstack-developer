Port Macquarie Ironman, 1st May 2016

Thanks everyone for the kind words of support, congratulatory messages and for following every updates, thanks Jen as well as Mango for your countless cheers, it got us through. And of course to our wives who where there before the start, up until everyone finished, to make sure we get the best 'pictures' :) as well as shouting the loudest cheers. Port Macquarie bike course is honest and tough, add the rain and it became a brutal course. There were a few crashes that I saw, and I too had a near slip as my rear wheel skidded going up Matthew Flinders drive. I managed to recover luckily. The run course was wet, some sections looked like a tough mudder race with all the mud you had to go through. On the swim, due to low tide, one section was too shallow that we had to walk, but overall it was good, especially the weir crossing. Everyone finished safely, very painful legs for a few days but the finisher medal is well deserved as we worked hard for it today. - Rom Faro

Melbourne Ironman, 22nd March 2015

Marlon Malinao and Rommel Faro became the first official FilOz members to tackle and finish the gruelling Melbourne Ironman. Debuting in 2012, IRONMAN Melbourne has since attracted the world's finest competitors. Athletes begin their day in the town of Frankston, south of Melbourne. The one-loop, 2.4-mile swim course leaves from the south side of the Frankston pier and heads north, before turning south to finish on the opposite side of the pier. The 112-mile, two-lap bike course incorporates the Eastlink Tollway, a world-class expressway closed exclusively for the use of competitors on race day. The iconic point-to-point run course also starts in Frankston, and concludes in St. Kilda, a funky beachside neighborhood with views of the Melbourne skyline.
